What is AAVE Bridge

Cross-Chain Transfers Built on Aave

AAVE Bridge lets you move supported assets between Aave markets on different blockchains. It uses Chainlink's Cross-Chain Interoperability Protocol (CCIP) to lock tokens on the source chain and mint or unlock them on the destination chain.

With Aave V4's hub-and-spoke architecture, liquidity is unified across all supported networks. No fragmented pools, no manual juggling between chains — just direct, verifiable transfers backed by the largest DeFi lending protocol.

Whether you're bridging ETH, stablecoins, or GHO, every transfer is secured by battle-tested smart contracts and Chainlink's decentralized oracle network.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

AAVE Bridge is a protocol tool that lets users move supported assets between Aave markets on different blockchains, such as Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, and Avalanche. It works by locking tokens on the source chain and unlocking or minting them on the destination chain, using secure cross-chain messaging via Chainlink CCIP.

You can bridge assets between Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, and Avalanche using AAVE Bridge. These networks are currently supported for both sending and receiving transfers.

Typical transfers take from a few minutes up to an hour, depending on network congestion and the chains involved. Transfers between Ethereum and Layer 2s like Arbitrum or Base are usually faster than between Ethereum and Avalanche.

Expect two types of costs: a bridge fee (often around 0.05% to 0.1% of the transferred amount) and gas fees on both the source and destination chains. Gas costs vary by network — Ethereum tends to be more expensive, while Arbitrum, Base, and Avalanche transactions are typically cheaper.

AAVE Bridge uses Chainlink CCIP, a widely-audited cross-chain protocol, to secure messages and asset transfers. The underlying smart contracts are also regularly audited, reducing risk, but like all DeFi tools, bridging is not completely without risk — review audits and best practices before large transfers.

Yes, you can bridge GHO between supported networks through AAVE Bridge. GHO uses a lock-and-mint model: tokens are locked on the source chain, and an equivalent amount is minted on the destination chain, ensuring supply remains balanced across chains.
